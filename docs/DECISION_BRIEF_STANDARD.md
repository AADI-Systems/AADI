# Decision Brief Standard (DBS)

Version: 1.0 (Draft)

---

# Purpose

The Decision Brief Standard (DBS) defines the minimum structure and quality requirements for every Decision Brief generated by AADI.

Its purpose is not to make decisions.

Its purpose is to prepare people to make better decisions.

Every Decision Brief should reduce cognitive load, improve understanding, and increase decision readiness.

---

# Design Philosophy

A Decision Brief must answer one question:

> Am I ready to make this decision?

If the answer is "No", the Decision Brief should clearly explain:

- What information is missing
- What risks remain
- What evidence is insufficient
- What should be prepared next

---

# Core Principles

Every Decision Brief must be:

- Explainable
- Evidence-Based
- Actionable
- Transparent
- Human-Centered
- Prioritized
- Trustworthy

---

# Standard Structure

Every Decision Brief consists of the following sections.

---

## 1. Metadata

Purpose:

Basic information about this Decision Brief.

Fields:

- ID
- Title
- Author (AI)
- Created Time
- Updated Time
- Version
- Priority
- Confidence

---

## 2. Executive Summary

Purpose:

Provide a concise overview.

Answer:

"What is happening?"

Length:

3~10 sentences.

---

## 3. Situation Assessment

Purpose:

Describe the current situation objectively.

Include:

- Context
- Background
- Current status
- Recent changes

No recommendations.

Only facts.

---

## 4. Key Findings

Purpose:

Highlight the most important discoveries.

Each finding should include:

- Finding
- Why it matters
- Impact

---

## 5. Priority Assessment

Purpose:

Identify what deserves attention first.

Include:

Priority Level

Possible values:

- Critical
- High
- Medium
- Low

Reason for prioritization.

---

## 6. Evidence

Purpose:

Support every important statement.

Evidence may include:

- Documents
- Reports
- Emails
- News
- GitHub
- Database
- Knowledge Base
- AI Analysis

Every major conclusion should have supporting evidence.

---

## 7. Reasoning

Purpose:

Explain how conclusions were reached.

Include:

- Logical reasoning
- Assumptions
- Dependencies
- Alternative interpretations

Reasoning should always be explainable.

---

## 8. Risk Assessment

Purpose:

Identify uncertainty.

Include:

- Known risks
- Unknown risks
- Assumptions
- Confidence impact

Possible severity:

Critical

High

Medium

Low

---

## 9. Options

Purpose:

Describe available choices.

Each option should include:

- Description
- Advantages
- Disadvantages
- Risks
- Estimated impact

No final recommendation.

---

## 10. Recommended Preparation

Purpose:

Prepare the user before making a decision.

Examples:

Collect additional data.

Consult stakeholders.

Verify assumptions.

Review regulations.

Run simulations.

Prepare contingency plans.

---

## 11. Missing Information

Purpose:

List unknown information.

Examples:

Missing documents.

Incomplete data.

Conflicting evidence.

Unverified assumptions.

---

## 12. Confidence

Purpose:

Measure confidence.

Suggested scale:

0~100%

Factors:

Evidence quality

Evidence quantity

Reasoning consistency

Information completeness

---

## 13. References

Purpose:

List all information sources.

Include:

Documents

URLs

Reports

Emails

Database records

Knowledge Base

GitHub repositories

---

# Quality Checklist

Every Decision Brief should answer:

✓ What happened?

✓ Why does it matter?

✓ What deserves attention?

✓ What evidence supports this?

✓ What risks exist?

✓ What options exist?

✓ What should be prepared next?

✓ What information is missing?

✓ How confident is this analysis?

---

# What a Decision Brief is NOT

A Decision Brief is NOT:

- A summary
- A chatbot response
- A generated article
- A search result
- An AI opinion

A Decision Brief prepares decisions.

It does not replace them.

---

# Expected Outcome

A successful Decision Brief should help users:

Understand the situation.

Reduce cognitive load.

Identify priorities.

Understand risks.

Gain confidence.

Prepare for a better decision.

The final decision always belongs to people.

---
