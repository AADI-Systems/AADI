# Decision Intelligence

Version: 1.0  
Date: July 2026  

> Decision Intelligence is the discipline of preparing better decisions through structured understanding, reasoning, and risk awareness.

---

# 1. Overview

Decision Intelligence is the foundation of AADI.

AADI does not focus on generating answers.

AADI focuses on improving decision preparation.

The purpose of Decision Intelligence is to help humans:

- Understand situations.
- Identify important factors.
- Evaluate risks.
- Explore options.
- Prepare before making decisions.

---

# 2. The Difference Between Information and Decision Intelligence

Information answers:

> What happened?

Decision Intelligence answers:

> What does it mean, why does it matter, and what should be prepared?

---

Traditional information systems focus on:

- Collecting data.
- Searching information.
- Summarizing content.

AADI focuses on:

- Understanding context.
- Connecting evidence.
- Evaluating uncertainty.
- Preparing decisions.

---

# 3. AADI Decision Preparation Model

AADI follows this workflow:


Information

↓

Understanding

↓

Reasoning

↓

Priority

↓

Risk Assessment

↓

Option Evaluation

↓

Decision Brief

↓

Decision Readiness

↓

Human Decision


---

# 4. Information Layer

The first stage is collecting relevant information.

Sources may include:

- Documents
- Reports
- Data
- External information
- Enterprise knowledge

The objective is not collecting everything.

The objective is identifying decision-relevant information.

---

# 5. Understanding Layer

Information must be transformed into understanding.

AADI analyzes:

- Context
- Relationships
- Trends
- Important changes
- Key signals

The goal:

Move from information availability to situation awareness.

---

# 6. Reasoning Layer

Reasoning is the core capability of AADI.

AADI helps users explore:

- Why something happened.
- What factors influence outcomes.
- What assumptions exist.
- What patterns can be identified.

Reasoning is not prediction.

Reasoning is structured thinking support.

---

# 7. Priority Layer

Not all information has equal importance.

AADI helps identify:

- What matters most.
- What requires attention.
- What should be investigated further.

Priority reduces cognitive load.

---

# 8. Risk Assessment Layer

Every decision contains uncertainty.

AADI helps identify:

- Known risks.
- Unknown risks.
- Missing information.
- Possible negative outcomes.

The goal is not eliminating uncertainty.

The goal is improving awareness before decisions.

---

# 9. Option Evaluation Layer

Good decisions require understanding alternatives.

AADI supports:

- Comparing options.
- Understanding trade-offs.
- Identifying consequences.
- Preparing questions.

AADI does not select the final option.

Humans remain responsible for judgment.

---

# 10. Decision Brief Generation

The output of the Decision Intelligence process is a Decision Brief.

A Decision Brief transforms analysis into decision preparation.

It provides:

- Situation understanding.
- Important evidence.
- Reasoning.
- Risks.
- Options.
- Preparation guidance.

Detailed standard:

See:

`DECISION_BRIEF_STANDARD.md`

---

# 11. Human-in-the-Loop Principle

AADI follows:


AI prepares

↓

Human decides


AI provides:

- Structure
- Analysis
- Context
- Preparation

Human provides:

- Judgment
- Experience
- Values
- Accountability

---

# 12. Decision Readiness

The final goal of AADI is not automation.

The goal is:

> Decision Readiness.

A user is decision-ready when they understand:

- The situation.
- The important factors.
- The risks.
- The available options.
- The remaining uncertainty.

---

# 13. What AADI Does Not Do

AADI does not:

- Make decisions for humans.
- Replace professional judgment.
- Guarantee correct outcomes.
- Remove uncertainty completely.

---

# 14. Core Principles

## Principle 1

Better decisions start with better preparation.

---

## Principle 2

Reasoning is more important than information volume.

---

## Principle 3

Understanding comes before action.

---

## Principle 4

Human judgment remains central.

---

## Principle 5

Technology should serve decision quality.

---

# 15. Future Evolution

Future AADI capabilities may include:

- Advanced reasoning workflows.
- Domain-specific decision models.
- Organizational decision memory.
- Collaborative decision preparation.
- Continuous learning from decision outcomes.

However:

The fundamental goal remains unchanged:

> Help humans prepare better decisions.
